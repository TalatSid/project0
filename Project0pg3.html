Special needs? Mm mmm Gifted perhaps

I do not agree with the term "Special needs" when one is speaking about kids with varying developmental abilities or what is described as being on Spectrum, just because he/she thinks/processes/analyzes differently. When a kid performs academically better than his or her peers the kid gets the "gifted" title. I think all the kids should have thal lable to redefine norm. The focus in the (currently segregated) two cases is customized developmental plan in the areas of excellence or need.

Below is a comparison of how the two groups are studied, there by enabling, if I may go as far as to say "discriminated"

Lable Focus areas
Gifted
  1. Diversity in possible fields of excellence
  2. Measures are always positive progress intensive
  3. Ease in finding information and resources
  4. Abundance of progress tracking tools
Special needs
  1. Focus on only 5 areas of development
  2. Failures are usually the highlights and possibility of accomplishments in other areas is overlooked
  3. A few online resources, that generalize the whole situation
  4. None
